Why We Are Here

Making sure no child struggles alone after school.

Every child deserves help with their homework. But many single mothers in Hong Kong work long hours to support their families, leaving children with no one at home who can guide them through English assignments. We are here to bridge that gap.

I

Busy Parents

Many single mothers in Hong Kong work 12-hour shifts. Because they are working during after-school study hours, children are often left to face complicated English homework on their own.

II

The Language Barrier

School curriculums require kids to write and speak fluent English. But parents who didn't grow up speaking English can't always help. We explain English grammar in Cantonese so kids actually understand.

III

A Helping Hand That Lasts

Many volunteer programs stop during exam periods or holidays. We have a dedicated team of student tutors from international schools, backed by free learning software so kids get help 24/7.

Our Easy Learning Method

We Explain English in Cantonese

Tutoring only in English can be super confusing for beginners. But simply translating words into Cantonese doesn't build long-term fluency either.

That's why we explain grammar clearly in Cantonese first, then immediately practice writing and speaking in English. This makes learning natural, fun, and easy!

Easy Tutoring Steps:

We explain grammar in Cantonese → The student understands the concept → We practice worksheets together in English → Success!

Free Learning App (GoAsk):

When tutors aren't around, kids can use our free GoAsk learning app on their phones. They just take a picture of their homework and get clear Cantonese explanations instantly 24/7.

How We Make It Happen

We believe in real action and transparent progress. From organizing volunteers to setting up free apps for families, here is how we systematically run our tutoring program.

Systematic Action Stages (CoV Program)

Stage Scope of Activities
1. Inventory & Investigation Researching root causes, contacting local community centers, establishing linguistic baselines.
2. Preparation & Planning Securing partnerships, building Cantonese-explanation sheets, recruiting student peers.
3. Action Logging 100+ hours, coordinating tutor rotations, deploying initial GoAsk licenses to underfunded households.
4. Reflection Refining grammar scaffolding sheets, tracking student progress, monitoring software usage trends.
5. Demonstration Presenting program outcomes, formalizing educational technology license grants, and receiving the Silver Volunteer Award.
